### Interesting fact
**Did you know that glass is actually a liquid, not a solid?** Although it feels solid, glass is technically a supercooled liquid. It doesn’t have a regular crystalline structure typical of true solids. Instead, the atoms in glass are arranged more randomly, similar to the arrangement in a liquid. This amorphous structure allows glass to flow very slowly, which can sometimes be observed in very old stained-glass windows where the glass is thicker at the bottom than at the top. This is because glass flows down very slowly under the influence of gravity, though it takes many centuries for this effect to become noticeable.
