### Interesting fact
**Did you know that the modern musical scale is deeply rooted in mathematics?** The twelve-tone equal temperament scale, which is the basis for modern Western music, divides the octave into twelve parts, all of which are logarithmically equal. This musical structure stems from a desire to create harmony and balance that allows for modulating between keys without any of them sounding out of tune relative to each other. This system was developed over centuries, but it was only fully realized and mathematically formalized in the 18th century by scientists and musicians, including the famous mathematician Leonhard Euler. This approach to tuning ensures that you can play in any key on a keyboard, and it will sound harmonious, all thanks to some very precise mathematical calculations!
