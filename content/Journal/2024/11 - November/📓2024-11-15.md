### Interesting fact
**Sieve of Eratosthenes** This ancient algorithm, named after the Greek mathematician Eratosthenes of Cyrene, is used to find all prime numbers up to a specified integer. Eratosthenes, who was the chief librarian at the Library of Alexandria, introduced this method in 240 BC. The Sieve of Eratosthenes works by iteratively marking the multiples of each prime number starting from 2. The numbers that remain unmarked are the primes. This simple yet efficient algorithm is still a fundamental concept taught in number theory and computer science today.
