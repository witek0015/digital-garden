### Interesting fact
**Did you know that the first programmable computer was conceptualized by Charles Babbage in the 1830s?** Called the Analytical Engine, it was designed to be a mechanical general-purpose computer and is considered by many as the first concept for a programmable computer. Babbage’s design included an arithmetic logic unit, control flow in the form of conditional branching and loops, and integrated memory, making it the first design for a complete computer architecture. However, the Analytical Engine was never completed in his lifetime. Ada Lovelace, often credited as the first computer programmer, wrote algorithms intended for implementation on the machine, highlighting its theoretical abilities long before modern computers were actually built.
