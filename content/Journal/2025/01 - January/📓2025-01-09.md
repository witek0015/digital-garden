### Interesting fact
**Did you know that the pistol shrimp can create a bubble hot enough to rival the sun’s surface?** When the shrimp snaps its claw shut at high speeds, it creates a high-pressure bubble that collapses to produce a shockwave, which can stun or kill prey. The temperature inside these bubbles briefly reaches over 4,700 degrees Celsius (about 8,500 degrees Fahrenheit), nearly matching the temperature at the surface of the sun. This remarkable mechanism is one of the most powerful and extreme acts in the animal kingdom, illustrating unique adaptations for survival and predation.
