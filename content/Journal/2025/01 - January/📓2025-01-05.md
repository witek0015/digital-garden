### Interesting fact
**Did you know that a teaspoon of a neutron star would weigh about 6 billion tons?** Neutron stars are the remnants of massive stars that have exploded in supernovae. Despite their small size—about 20 kilometers in diameter—they are incredibly dense. The gravity on a neutron star is so strong that if you were to drop a marshmallow from one meter high, it would hit the surface at about 7,200 kilometers per hour!
