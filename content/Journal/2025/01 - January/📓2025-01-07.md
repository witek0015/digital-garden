### Interesting fact
**Did you know that electric eels generate enough electricity to power small electronics?** Electric eels are known to produce powerful electric shocks of up to 600 volts, which they use to stun prey and deter predators. This capability arises from specialized cells called electrocytes that, when activated, work similarly to batteries connected in series. Researchers have studied these mechanisms to inspire new technologies in bioengineering and energy generation, looking at ways to harness similar bioelectric principles.
