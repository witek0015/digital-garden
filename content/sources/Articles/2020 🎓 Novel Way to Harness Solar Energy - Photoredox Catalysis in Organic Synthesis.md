# Notes
- This is a research paper published in the journal _Kinetics and Catalysis_ that reviews the growing field of visible-light photo-redox catalysis in organic synthesis.
- The authors discuss the fundamental principles of photo-redox catalysis, how it utilizes visible light to drive chemical transformations, and the diverse range of organic reactions it can mediate.
- They then detail recent advances in this field, categorized by reaction type, including *C–H functionalization*, *coupling reactions*, *cycloaddition*, *reduction*, *oxidation*, and *fluorination*.
- The review emphasizes the potential of this technology to **promote environmentally friendly and energy-efficient organic synthesis**, making it a valuable resource for researchers interested in this emerging field.

# Highlights
- Significant absorption in the visible spectrum is essential for photo-redox catalysts to accelerate the reaction by many folds. Generally, metallo- or organo-compounds capable of absorbing visible light, resulting in an excited state species thus can be employed as photo-redox catalysts
- There are different forms of the conjugated polymer photocatalysts like linear, graphitic C, N-based, porous, nanostructured, and coordinated polymers or polymers with units made of small molecular dye structures.
- Lang et al. made a conclusion that photoredox could bridge the gap between visible-light photo-redox catalysis and other types of redox catalysis such as transition-metal catalysis, biocatalysis or electrocatalysis while study on the various visible light photo-redox as renewable energy and green chemistry perspective.
- Courant and Masson reported visible light catalysis, a recent and very promising technological refinement of transformations. We can control the energy of visible light radiation using variety of (metallic or nonmetallic) systems for creating C–X, C–O, C–N, and C–C types of bonds under mild conditions and starting from unsaturated substrates
- Although photo-redox catalysis with transition metal complexes has only recently received widespread attention as a tool for synthetic organic chemists, it has already been applied to the development of a wide range of new carbon–carbon bond-forming reactions.
- Photo-redox system harnesses the energy of light to accelerate a chemical reaction via single-electron transfer reactions
- Visible light photo-redox catalyst (VLPC) i.e. a photocatalyst working for one-electron oxidation and reduction under visible light irradiation, receives much awareness, because there is possibility for use sunlight as an energy source.
- Photocatalysts are capable of absorbing visible light, which in turn lead to generation of an excited state species. At ambient temperature, the excited state species can donate or accept an electron from other substrates to mediate redox reactions with high efficiency.
- Catalytic reactions are classified according to the following two criteria: (i) electron transfer/energy transfer, (ii) intra and inter-molecular catalyst systems.
- These compounds were employed in the application of photo-redox catalyzed atom transfer radical polymerization (ATRP) for controlled polymer synthesis and small molecule transformations like trifluoromethylation and C–N and C–S cross-couplings.
- Reaction such as reductive couplings, photocycloadditions, intermolecular C–H functionalisation processes, fragmentations, and cyclisation catalyzed by light was reviewed
- VLPC enables reactive radical intermediates to be catalytically generated at ambient temperature and has vastly extended the range of substrates and reaction schemes that have been traditionally the domain of radical reactions.
- On the basis of the bibliometric study, it can be concluded that photo-redox catalysis strategy harness the solar energy, which is green, clean, abundant, and renewable source of energy to synthesis of vast spectrum of organic scaffolds.
