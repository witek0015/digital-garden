# Notes
- This 2020 review article, published in the _Asian Journal of Research and Reviews in Physics_, explores the crucial role of physics in advancing medical diagnosis and therapy, particularly in Nigeria. 
- The author, Gabriel Ankeli, highlights the significant impact of physics-based technologies, such as **computerized tomography (CT) scans, lasers, nanotechnology, and matter/antimatter collision imaging**, in addressing various health challenges including cancer, tumors, and infectious diseases like COVID-19. 
- The article emphasizes the need for increased **development and implementation of physics-based technologies** to improve healthcare standards and national development in Nigeria, advocating for further research, education, and training in medical physics.
# Highlights
- Physics plays a crucial role in medical science, enhancing healthcare standards and contributing to national development. Physics-based technologies have greatly improved the diagnosis and treatment of various health challenges.
- Several cutting-edge diagnostic techniques are discussed in the paper, including computerized tomography (CT) and X-ray lasers, nanotechnology, matter/antimatter collision imaging (PET scanning), and laser-induced breakdown spectroscopy (LIBS).
- Nanotechnology is rapidly advancing in medical applications, with potential in targeted drug delivery, radiotherapy and cancer treatment, nanobiosensors, and nanomedical imaging. For example, nanoparticles can be used to deliver drugs directly to diseased cells, minimizing damage to healthy cells.
- LIBS is a promising technique for diagnosing malignant cells and tissues due to its sensitivity, minimal invasiveness, and ability to detect trace elements in low concentrations.
- Medical physicists have made significant contributions to modern medicine, developing technologies like X-rays, MRI, and PET scanning. They are essential for administering radiation therapy and ensuring quality in both treatment and imaging.
- The author suggests encouraging new research, supporting the education of more medical physicists, and retraining existing personnel to further advance the field and address future healthcare challenges.
