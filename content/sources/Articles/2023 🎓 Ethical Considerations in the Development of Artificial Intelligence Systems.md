# Notes
- Bias in AI systems can perpetuate and amplify existing societal biases and discrimination. This can lead to unfair treatment of individuals, such as fewer women being hired for certain jobs or misidentification of individuals from certain racial groups by facial recognition systems.
- AI systems raise privacy concerns as they collect and analyze vast amounts of personal data. If this data is not handled appropriately, it can be used for unintended purposes, such as identity theft, or be accessed by unauthorized individuals in data breaches.
- Accountability is a significant ethical concern surrounding AI systems, as their complex algorithms and machine-learning models make it difficult to understand how they make decisions. Determining who is responsible for their actions and decisions, especially when AI systems become more autonomous, is a significant challenge.
- Transparency is crucial for AI systems to be ethical. AI systems need to be able to explain their decisions and actions. This allows people to understand why a particular decision was made, to evaluate the fairness and reliability of the system, and to identify potential biases and discrimination.

# Highlights
- AI refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence such as visual perception, speech recognition, decisionmaking, and language translation
- AI systems can be biased in many ways such as racial, gender, and cultural biases. For instance, AI algorithms used in hiring processes may exhibit gender bias, resulting in fewer women being hired for certain jobs
- The ethical concerns around bias in AI are two-fold: first, biased AI systems can perpetuate and amplify existing societal biases and discrimination, and second, the use of biased AI systems can lead to unfair treatment of individuals. For example, facial recognition systems have been shown to have higher error rates for individuals with darker skin tones than for those with lighter skin tones
- Another privacy concern related to AI is the potential for data breaches (Murdoch, 2021). If personal data is not adequately protected, it can be accessed by unauthorized individuals, leading to identity theft and other forms of fraud. This is particularly concerning given the sensitive nature of the data collected by many AI systems, such as health data and financial information.
- The development and deployment of AI has the potential to bring about significant benefits for individuals and society as a whole, but it also presents a range of ethical challenges and concerns. The studies discussed the ethical considerations surrounding the development and use of AI in developed and African countries
