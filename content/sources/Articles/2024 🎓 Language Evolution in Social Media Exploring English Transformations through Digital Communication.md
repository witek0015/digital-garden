# Notes
- This research article, published in _Library Progress International_, investigates the evolution of the English language within the context of social media. 
- Utilizing Howard Giles' Communication Accommodation Theory (CAT) as its framework, the study analyzes how factors like technological advancements, social media platforms, and globalization have shaped English usage online. 
- The authors explore linguistic changes such as the rise of abbreviations, emojis, and neologisms, alongside the impact of features like autocorrect.
- Ultimately, the research aims to demonstrate the strong interdependence between language and social media, highlighting the dynamic adaptation of English in the digital age and its implications for communication professionals and educators.

# Highlights
- The English language has evolved significantly over time, influenced by social, cultural, and technological factors. For instance, the Norman Conquest in 1066 led to the absorption of French influences into the English vocabulary.
- The Communication Accommodation Theory (CAT) explains how individuals adjust their language to converge with or diverge from their communication partners. Convergence involves adapting one's communication style to become more similar to others, while divergence emphasizes differences.
- Social media platforms have significantly impacted language evolution in the digital age. They have facilitated the development of new words and expressions and influenced communication styles.
- Social media platforms have given rise to distinctive linguistic features such as emojis, abbreviations, acronyms, hashtags, slang, and memes. These elements contribute to the dynamic and evolving nature of language in the digital sphere.
- The relationship between language and social media is interdependent. Without language, communication on social media would be impossible. Conversely, the absence of social media would likely result in fewer online-specific linguistic innovations.
- Social media has fostered global multilingualism and diversity by connecting people from various linguistic backgrounds. This interaction has led to the mixing of languages and the borrowing of terms, enriching the linguistic landscape.
