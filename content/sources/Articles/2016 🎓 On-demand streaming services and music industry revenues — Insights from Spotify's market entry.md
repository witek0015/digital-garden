# Notes
- This academic paper investigates the impact of on-demand music streaming services (like Spotify) on music industry revenue. 
- The authors used a longitudinal study of over 2500 German music consumers to analyze the effects of both free and paid streaming services, specifically focusing on **cannibalization** (whether streaming reduces purchases of other music formats). Their findings reveal that while both free and paid streaming cannibalize existing sales, **paid streaming generates a net positive revenue effect for the industry**, while the effect of free streaming is positive only for previously inactive consumers. 
- Overall, the study concludes that the positive impact of paid streaming outweighs the negative impact of free streaming, resulting in a **net positive effect on overall industry revenue** in their analyzed context.

# Highlights
- The adoption of free and paid music streaming services results in cannibalization of music purchases from other channels. Consumers who adopted a free streaming service spent 10.8% less on music purchases after adopting, while those who adopted a paid service reduced their spending by 24%.
- Despite cannibalization, paid streaming services have a positive net effect on music industry revenue. This is due to the subscription fees paid by users outweighing the reduction in purchases from other channels. The effect was positive regardless of the user's pre-adoption spending levels.
- Free streaming services, however, may have an overall negative impact on revenue, especially for users who were active music buyers before adoption. For previously inactive users, free streaming services have a positive effect on revenue.
- At an aggregate level, the positive revenue from paid streaming services compensates for the negative impact of free streaming services, leading to a net positive effect on the music industry.
- To increase the profitability of free streaming services, music labels could:
	- Increase the payout rate per stream.
	- Attract a higher proportion of previously inactive music consumers.
	- Reduce the utility of free services to encourage conversion to paid subscriptions.
- Artists should negotiate contracts that adequately reflect the growing importance of streaming revenue, as traditional revenue sources decline with the rise of streaming.
- Retailers operating purchase-based music businesses should consider offering streaming services to adapt to the shifting landscape of the music industry.
