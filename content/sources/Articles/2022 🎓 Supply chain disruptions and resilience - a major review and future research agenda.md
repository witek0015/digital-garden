# Notes
-  The authors conduct a comprehensive review of literature published over the past 20 years in prominent journals, highlighting the latest trends and developments. 
- They synthesize the findings into eight key subsections: types of disruptions, the ripple effect, impact analysis of SC disruptions, resilience-response-recovery strategies, popular quantitative approaches for SC disruptions, cost-benefit analysis of resilience, IT tools for enhanced resilience, and finally, a detailed research agenda for future research. 
- The primary goal of the paper is to amalgamate existing knowledge about supply chain disruptions, provide insights for decision-makers, and identify research gaps that require further investigation.

# Highlights
- Supply chain disruptions have become increasingly common and impactful in recent years. Supply chains have become more complex and interdependent, which has increased the risk of disruptions. Disruptions can create a snowball effect, with serious consequences for all related supply chain echelons.
-   There are many types of supply chain disruptions, but they can be broadly categorized by their frequency of occurrence. Catastrophic events, such as natural disasters and terrorist attacks, are low-frequency but high-impact events. Infrastructural events, such as production downtime and supplier delays, are high-frequency but low-impact events.
- Supply chain disruptions can have a significant impact on businesses, including financial losses, customer dissatisfaction, and damage to brand reputation
- Companies can take steps to build resilience into their supply chains and mitigate the impact of disruptions. These steps include building redundancy, developing contingency plans, and adopting information and communication technology (ICT)
- There is a growing body of research on supply chain disruptions and resilience. This research has helped to advance theoretical debates and influence decision-making. However, there are still many research gaps that need to be addressed
- The future of supply chain management will be shaped by the need to build resilience to disruptions. This will require new principles and models to support supply chain management
- New digital technologies, such as the Internet of Things (IoT), big data analytics, and machine learning, can help to mitigate disruption risks. These technologies can enable companies to better monitor their supply chains, predict disruptions, and respond more quickly to incidents
