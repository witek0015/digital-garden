# Notes
- This research paper investigates the impact of cryptocurrencies on the global financial system, specifically focusing on volatility spillovers. 
- The authors analyze the transmission of shocks and volatility between the cryptocurrency market and traditional asset classes (equities and bonds) using data from January 2018 to March 2020. 
- Employing a sophisticated econometric model (BEKK-GARCH), they find that while cryptocurrency market returns influence other markets, volatility spillovers primarily flow _from_ traditional assets _into_ the cryptocurrency market, suggesting the latter acts more as a recipient than an originator of volatility. 
- The study concludes that cryptocurrencies, despite their high volatility and low correlation with other assets, are now integral to the global financial system and warrant careful consideration by investors and regulators alike.

# Highlights
- The Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR) is characterized by the growing use of technology, cloud computing, Internet of things (IoT), and artificial intelligence, etc. This, followed by the introduction of new technology driven financial products, such as Cryptocurrencies, has resulted into a new era of financial disruption, with profound socioeconomic consequences that have trickled across the globe
- A volatility spillover occurs when unexpected shocks and/or volatility in one market triggers and transmits volatility in other markets
- two hypotheses on how volatility transmission can be manifested across markets.
	- The first one is the “heat wave” concept, that suggests that volatility is market specific. That is to say that a day of volatility in a market is expected to be followed by another volatile day in the same market.
	- the second concept, known as the “meteor wave” suggests the presence of volatility spillovers between the markets, i.e., volatility in one market today, may be followed by a volatile day in another market tomorrow. This transmission of volatility between markets is generally believed to be the result of increased integration, and also due to the presence of financial contagion
- speculative bubbles in the cryptocurrency markets, may end up spreading contagion and consequently the financial stability can deteriorate due to this.
- The situation in the Investment Grade Bond market is different, where the negative coefficient of the two period lagged returns in CRY indicates that the decline in the price may be due to the higher yields that are demanded by the investors, in anticipation of increased systemic risk.
- cryptos have low correlation with other asset classes, but the standard deviation of the returns is also very high. This essentially indicates the high volatile structure of the cryptocurrencies.
- returns in cryptocurrencies effect the returns of other segments as well
