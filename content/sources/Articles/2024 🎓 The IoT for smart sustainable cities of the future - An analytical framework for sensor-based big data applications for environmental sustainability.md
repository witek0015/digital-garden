# Notes
- This research paper investigates the application of the Internet of Things (IoT) and its associated big data analytics to enhance environmental sustainability in smart sustainable cities. 
- The author argues that current smart city initiatives often overlook the environmental dimension of sustainability, focusing primarily on economic and social aspects. 
- The paper uses a thematic analysis of existing literature to propose an analytical framework that integrates sensor technologies, data processing platforms (like cloud and fog computing, Hadoop), and various data-centric applications across multiple urban domains (transport, energy, environment, etc.). 
- The ultimate goal is to demonstrate how leveraging sensor-based big data from the IoT can significantly improve environmental sustainability within future smart cities, despite the challenges inherent in data management, privacy, and security.
# Highlights
- The article underscores the growing importance of the Internet of Things (IoT) and big data analytics in creating smart sustainable cities, particularly in enhancing environmental sustainability.
- The article points out a gap in existing research, which primarily focuses on the economic and quality-of-life aspects of smart cities, often neglecting their role in promoting environmental sustainability.
- Pervasive sensing and computing are highlighted as crucial elements for collecting, measuring, and processing vast amounts of urban data related to the environment.
- The article proposes an analytical framework that integrates sensor-based big data applications with urban systems and domains, emphasizing the role of cloud computing and fog/edge computing in data processing and management.
- The framework outlines various data-centric applications enabled by the IoT that can advance environmental sustainability, including smart transportation, smart energy, smart environment, and smart buildings.
- The author stresses the need to combine the concepts of smart cities and sustainable cities, leveraging IoT and big data analytics to drive the transition toward environmentally sustainable development.
- The article acknowledges challenges associated with IoT and big data analytics in smart sustainable cities, including data storage and processing, privacy, security, and ethical considerations
