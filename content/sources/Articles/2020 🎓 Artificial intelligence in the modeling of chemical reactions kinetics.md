# Notes
- The author highlights the challenges of traditional kinetic modeling, which often require complex mathematical equations and extensive experimental data. 
- AI methods offer a promising alternative by learning from data and generating predictions without relying on explicit equations. 
- The paper then dives into several recent studies showcasing the application of AI methods for various chemical processes, such as catalytic cracking, photochemical reactions, laminar and turbulent combustion, enzymatic reactions, and even quantum chemistry. 
- The author discusses the advantages and limitations of these AI approaches, emphasizing their potential to accelerate reaction analysis, optimize reactor design, and uncover hidden patterns within complex reaction systems. 
- However, the paper also acknowledges the need for large datasets for training AI models and the ongoing research required to further refine and enhance these techniques. 
- Overall, the paper provides an overview of the current state of AI in chemical kinetics, highlighting its promising potential for future advancement in this field.

- From a design point of view, kinetics is of fundamental importance when estimating the volume and/or shape of the chemical reactor. Also the knowledge of kinetics is particularly important when designing reactor control systems.
- From the industrial point of view, the kinetics of the chemical reaction is a key data necessary for design, analysis and optimization tasks. In classical design methods, the knowledge of reaction kinetics allows to determine the required volume of reaction volumes, either in zero-dimensional approach as for a tank reactor or in one-dimensional approach in case of column or tubular apparatus.
