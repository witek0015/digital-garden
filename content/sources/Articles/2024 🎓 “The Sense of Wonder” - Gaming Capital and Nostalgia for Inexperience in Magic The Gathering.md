# Highlights
- Nostalgia in Magic: The Gathering (MTG) is linked to a player's gaming capital. As players gain expertise, they paradoxically develop nostalgia for when they had less knowledge and a greater "sense of wonder".
- The trading card game Magic: The Gathering (MTG) is a constantly evolving game where players adapt to new cards, increasing their knowledge and gaming capital.
- Gaming capital includes expertise in a game's rules, lore, and culture. It changes how players experience the game, making past experiences "irretrievable"
- The study uses qualitative interviews with Australian Magic: The Gathering players to explore how gaming capital leads to nostalgia for inexperience.
- MTG facilitates in-person play at specialized retailers and online play using Magic: The Gathering: Arena (MTGA). The different rulesets or "formats" available shape subcommunities within the overarching MTG cultural sphere.
- Cards in MTG feature artwork of characters, landscapes and magic spells inspired by real-world mythological corpuses, preexisting fantasy literature, and tropes. The game has expanded into a "multiverse" of worlds drawing on themes such as Japanese feudalism, gothic horror, 1920s gangster culture and sixteenth-century colonization.
- Nostalgia has evolved from describing homesickness to a yearning for the past. Boym's framework differentiates between restorative nostalgia (desire to return to the past) and reflective nostalgia (nostalgia for the past's place in one's life journey).
- Nostalgia is important in the MTG sphere, with players wanting to revisit and reflect on old play patterns. This has led to a collector's market and community-managed formats.
- Gaming capital is generated when players learn by playing or consuming paratexts, which in turn shape future experiences.
- Acquiring gaming capital can remove potential ways to experience games. Prior experience prevents engagement as a total novice.
- The study used semi-structured interviews and photo-elicitation to gather data, asking participants about their play histories, opinions on MTG, and understanding of nostalgia. Participants supplied a photograph of a nostalgic MTG-related object.
- Gaming capital is predicated on ownership of cards and physical peripherals and powerful cards are any that provide a significant gameplay advantage.
- The sharing of strategic knowledge can tighten community bonds through mutual exchange of capital.
- The transition from novice to master eliminates the early sense of open possibility and players are incapable of returning to the (in)experience level of those early forays.
- Financial gaming capital impacts players' agency, with the secondary market and Reserved List restricting access for those who cannot afford expensive cards.
- Players experience nostalgia for past times when financial limitations were less constraining.
- Accumulation of knowledge creates nostalgia for an irreclaimable past. Early experiences are sentimentalized as players realize their knowledge forms constraints on play.
- The acquisition of gaming capital shrinks the horizon of possibilities to linear paths of deterministic action.
- The author acknowledges the limitations of being an "acafan" and suggests that further research from MTG outsiders would be beneficial.
# Notes
Ach... I remember the first MTG pack I opened, from the Theros: Beyond Death set. It was late 2019 I think, and Ikoria was just about to release. There was no play boosters, only the regular draft ones and my rare was a crappy Eat to Extiction card, a black removal that exiled a creature but was overpriced in terms of mana... But the fact that I remember it must mean something. Looking back, I am really unhappy that I was stripped of this sense of MTG wonder, since Oskar introduced me to online databases and deck-building sites as a first thing. Unfortunate, since I did not get to experience the magic of lack of knowledge about the game. But now, when I go to limited events I shy from checking cards spoiled for the set for this exact reason... A sense of wonder, I could call it. Thinking that damn, this card is a bomb... Damn, it's a shame that it is so hard to dodge spoilers due to them being all over my social media. 
Anyways, reading about other's definition of nostalgia in my favourite game made me really glad and happy.
