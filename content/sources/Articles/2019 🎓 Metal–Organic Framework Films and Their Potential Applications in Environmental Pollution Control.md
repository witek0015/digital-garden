# Notes
- This research account details the development of metal-organic framework (MOF) films for environmental remediation. 
- The authors highlight challenges in processing brittle MOF particles into practical devices and present innovative fabrication methods, including postsynthetic polymerization and solvent-free hot-pressing, to create robust and flexible MOF films. 
- These films, termed MOFilters, demonstrate high efficiency in capturing particulate matter (PM) and removing harmful gases like SO2 and O3 from air, and toxic ions from water. 
- The study emphasizes the scalability of these techniques, showcasing a roll-to-roll production method for large-scale MOF film manufacturing, furthering the practical applications of MOFs in environmental pollution control.

# Highlights
- Metal−organic frameworks (MOFs), constructed from metal cations or clusters and organic ligands, are an intriguing new class of crystalline hybrid materials with reticular topology
- versatile functionalities make MOFs very attractive candidates for gas storage, molecule separation, drug delivery, heterogeneous catalysis, sensing
- According to Fischer and co-workers,15 MOF films can be divided into two main categories, surface-mounted MOFs (SURMOFs) and polycrystalline films. The former is a kind of ultrathin and smooth MOF film newly emerging.19 Highly ordered, preferentially oriented, and thickness-tunable SURMOFs can be elaborately designed and achieved under carefully controlled conditions by using methods like the Langmuir−Blodgett technique and liquid phase epitaxy
- Generally, two main concepts are introduced, including post-treatment of the performed MOF particles and in situ growth of MOF films.
- Direct growth of MOF nanocrystals on the given surface is a straightforward and useful strategy to obtain MOF coatings.14,31−35 However, some commonly used techniques, like solvothermal synthesis, usually could not lead to a firm bond between MOF layers and substrates. Also these methods may be time or energy consuming from the view of practical application.
- Flexible and conductive MOF films, serving as electrodes for high-performance solidstate supercapacitors (SSC), were prepared by developing an in situ interweaving method.36 Briefly, isolated and insulating MOF crystals resting on a flexible substrate were electrochemically interwoven by intrinsically conducting polymers. The fabrication of an MOF-based electrode was a two-step process
	- First, ZIF-67 selected as an electrical charge storing material, was uniformly coated on the carbon cloth (CC) in the presence of Super P and a poly(vinylidene fluoride) (PVDF) binder
	- conductive polyaniline (PANI) was then synthesized from monomer aniline via an electrochemical process to interconnect nonconductive ZIF-67 crystals
- The MOF-based foams reported are usually limited to bulky bodies with only one type of MOF incorporated. The advantages of MOFs in structural and functional diversity are not efficiently explored, especially in mixed-component applications
- Direct self-assembly of MOF precursors on the given surface is a straightforward and very promising strategy for the fabrication of functional MOF coating layers. It can be achieved by methods like solvothermal synthesis, electrochemical deposition, low-temperature self-assembly, etc.
- Roll-to-roll processing, a mass production technique for MOF coatings, has been developed from the solvent-free HoP method
- PM pollution is one of the major concerns around the world, especially in developing countries. MOFs with unsaturated metal sites and defects are thought to be capable of removing highly polar PM due to their electrostatic interactions
