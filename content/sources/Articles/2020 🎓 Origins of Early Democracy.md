# Notes
- They argue that **early democracy, characterized by governance through councils where rulers sought consent from the population, evolved independently in a wide range of human societies**.
- The paper finds a **statistically significant correlation between council governance and high variation in agricultural suitability**, supporting the argument that information asymmetries regarding local production incentivized rulers to share power with councils.
- . The authors further explore alternative mechanisms for dealing with information asymmetries, such as the emergence of bureaucracies, and find evidence that **bureaucracies can act as a substitute for council governance**.
