# Notes
- This is a peer-reviewed article reviewing recent advancements in perovskite solar cells (PSCs). 
- The authors explore the **structure and key properties** of PSCs, highlighting their **high efficiency**, **low fabrication costs**, and **tunability**. 
- However, the review also emphasizes the **significant challenges** hindering large-scale commercialization, namely **stability issues** related to moisture sensitivity and the need for **scalable manufacturing techniques** beyond laboratory-scale spin-coating. 
- Finally, the paper outlines promising future directions, including the development of **lead-free PSCs** and **high-efficiency multi-junction cells** to overcome current limitations.

# Highlights
- Perovskite solar cells (PSCs) are a promising new generation of solar cells that have rapidly increased in power conversion efficiency (PCE) from 3.5% to 25.8% in a decade. PSCs offer advantages such as low fabrication costs and high tunability compared to conventional silicon-based solar cells.
- The perovskite structure (ABX3) is highly tunable, allowing for the integration of elements with different valences. The A-site cation does not contribute to the band edge but impacts the material's deep energy levels. The choice of halide (X-site) influences the bandgap, with larger halides leading to a smaller bandgap.
- Stability is the biggest challenge facing PSC commercialization. Perovskite materials are sensitive to environmental factors like heat, light, moisture, and oxygen. The weak ionic bonds, hydrogen bonding, and van der Waals forces within the perovskite structure contribute to this vulnerability.
- Solution processing is the most common and cost-effective method for perovskite film synthesis. The one-step method involves spin-coating a pre-mixed solution of CH3NH3X and PbX2. While the two-step sequential method yields higher PCEs, the one-step method offers superior control over crystallinity and morphology.
- Scalability is another key challenge for PSC commercialization. Laboratory-scale spin-coating techniques are not suitable for large-scale production due to inconsistencies in film thickness, material waste, and incompatibility with roll-to-roll processing. Industrial-scale techniques like screen printing and slot-die coating are more promising for large-scale manufacturing.
- Lead-free PSCs are being researched to address the toxicity of lead. Tin (Sn) is the most promising alternative, with PCEs reaching 10-12%. However, further research is needed to improve the efficiency and investigate potential toxicity from tin oxidation byproducts.
- Tandem and multijunction solar cells are being explored to further enhance PCE and address stability issues. Combining perovskite with silicon in a tandem structure has achieved a PCE of 29.15%. Tandem cells using 2D-perovskites as the high bandgap absorber hold potential for high PCE and stability.
