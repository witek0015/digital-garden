# Highlights
- Tabletop Role-Playing Games (TRPGs) are a significant cultural influence, shaping the evolution of the fantastic genre and its culture. TRPGs draw heavily from speculative fiction for their settings and inspire creators in the genre. Many successful authors, as well as video game developers, have cited TRPGs as an influence. The genre's popularity is further bolstered by successful tie-in products like novels, comics, video games, and movies. TRPG communities also contribute to the culture of the fantastic, ranging from large conventions to smaller, local groups formed for gameplay.
- TRPG settings evolve over time, reflecting societal changes and conflicts. TRPG designers are responsive to feedback from the community, incorporating changes that reflect the desires of players, leading to more diverse and socially conscious settings. Examples of this evolution can be seen in both Dungeons and Dragons and World of Darkness.
- The evolution of Dungeons and Dragons (D&D) shows a shift towards inclusivity and representation. While early editions relied heavily on tropes and stereotypes, later editions made conscious efforts to address issues of gender, race, and sexual orientation. For instance, the fifth edition of D&D directly addressed alternate gender expression, featured a woman of colour as the symbol for the human race, and included openly homosexual characters in published adventures.
- The evolution of World of Darkness (WoD) demonstrates a similar progression towards inclusivity. WoD differentiated itself from D&D by setting its games in the real world and tackling social issues like capitalism, organized religion, and the experiences of the marginalized. Supplements for WoD games provided detailed descriptions of various cities around the world, drawing from their actual history and culture rather than relying on stereotypes. However, early editions still contained problematic representations. Later editions of WoD continued to prioritize addressing social issues and became more inclusive.
- Published TRPG materials play a significant role in shaping player perceptions and expectations of the setting. While the game encourages player agency and creativity, game books and supplements influence the style of play and the types of stories that emerge. Narrative and cultural theories support this idea, highlighting the influence of setting, masterplots, and genre conventions on storytelling.
- TRPG narratives are collaborative, with players actively contributing to the development of the setting. This collaborative storytelling involves drawing on players' personal experiences, cultural backgrounds, and interpretations, leading to narratives that go beyond the initial material presented in game books. The interactive nature of TRPGs allows for a deeper engagement with the narrative compared to passive consumption of other media.
- The evolution of TRPG settings highlights the dynamic relationship between popular culture and societal values. TRPG settings adapt and change in response to evolving societal norms and the growing diversity of the player base. This suggests that studying TRPGs can provide insights into broader trends within the fantastic genre and popular culture as a whole.
