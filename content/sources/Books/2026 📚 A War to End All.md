*Michael L. Fletcher, Clayton W. Snyder*
### Review
_A War to End All_ is a fittingly unhinged finale to one of the most warped and original grimdark trilogies out there. Where _The Mirror’s Truth_ occasionally felt like it was staggering around in circles, this one **marches forward with brutal confidence**, thanks in large part to a clever structure and a tighter grip on multiple perspectives. The shifting POVs actually elevate the storytelling this time around, adding **layers of political manipulation, divine meddling, and very human madness** and making the narrative more complex but also far more rewarding to follow.

There are still a few uneven character arcs along the way, moments where certain players feel undercooked or sidelined. But then there's the one character — the unexpected **core of the book—who gets so much space, depth, and narrative weight** that it more than balances things out. Their arc becomes the emotional backbone of the whole chaotic mess, and it _works_.

You can also **feel Clayton Snyder’s influence** in the prose. The writing flows smoother, the transitions are cleaner, and despite the sheer madness of the plot, the book **reads effortlessly**. The collaboration seems to have injected fresh energy without losing Fletcher’s signature brand of nihilistic philosophy and reality-warping insanity.

As for the ending... it’s absolute. Final. Burnt bridges and smoking wreckage, with **no clear road left for sequels** (which is both bold and a bit bittersweet). Part of it does feel slightly underwhelming... maybe because expectations were sky-high, or because there's just no clean way to end something this fundamentally broken. Still, what we get is **bleak, wild, and honest to the world it built**.

In the end, _A War to End All_ delivers exactly what it promises: a **deranged, devastating conclusion** to a trilogy where reality was always subjective, sanity was negotiable, and hope was optional.
### Context
- The **final book in the _Manifest Delusions_ trilogy**, bringing the long-brewing madness to a brutal, bloody crescendo.
- Continues exploring a world where **delusions shape reality**, following surviving characters from previous books as they’re pulled into a war sparked by belief, insanity, and unchecked power.
- The story dives deeper into themes of **identity, faith, chaos, and the futility of control**, escalating everything: violence, philosophy, character insanity to its most extreme form.
- A dark, unhinged, and appropriately cataclysmic end to one of grimdark fantasy’s most original and deranged series.
