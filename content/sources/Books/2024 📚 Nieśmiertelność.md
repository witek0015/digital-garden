# [[2024 📚 Nieśmiertelność]]

## Context
-  - **Philosophical Context**: Kundera’s works are heavily influenced by existentialist and phenomenological philosophy. These philosophical traditions explore concepts such as the absurdity of human existence, the role of individual choice, the search for meaning, and the nature of being and time. Philosophers like Søren Kierkegaard, Friedrich Nietzsche, and Martin Heidegger, who deal with these subjects, provide a backdrop to understanding Kundera’s thematic explorations of identity, human life, and the metaphysical questions that pervade his writing.
- **Literary Context**: Kundera is often placed within the modernist and postmodernist literary movements, which are characterized by narrative experimentation, fragmentation, and a focus on subjective reality. Knowledge of these movements, along with familiarity with other European authors like Franz Kafka (who wrote about absurdity and bureaucracy) and Marcel Proust (known for his explorations of memory and time), can help you see how Kundera both fits into and reacts against these traditions.
- **Cultural and Political Context**: Understanding the political history of the Czech Republic during the 20th century, particularly the Communist era and the Prague Spring, is crucial. Kundera’s work often critiques the repressive regimes in Eastern Europe and the materialism of the West. His life in exile in France adds another layer, reflecting his personal and critical views on both Eastern and Western cultures and politics.
- **The Novel’s Structure and Style**: Kundera uses a non-linear narrative, authorial intrusions, and a blend of essay and fiction. His style often involves stepping back from the narrative to discuss philosophical ideas directly with the reader. This method breaks traditional storytelling forms and invites readers to engage in philosophical reflection.
- **Themes of the Novel**: _Immortality_ delves into the nature of the human condition and the desire for legacy. It questions how individuals seek to achieve a sense of immortality, whether through art, relationships, or influence over others. Themes of love, power, society, and the individual’s place within historical currents are also central, making the novel a rich tapestry of philosophical inquiry interwoven with narrative.
