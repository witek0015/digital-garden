### Context
- A Canadian author known for writing **grimdark fantasy** with psychological depth, brutal settings, and deeply flawed characters. Fletcher has a background in music and tech, which weirdly reflects in his structured chaos—his books are methodical in their madness.
- Published in **2015**, _Beyond Redemption_ is the first book in the **Manifest Delusions** series, a grimdark fantasy world where **belief defines reality**. The novel explores a **world ruled by delusion**, where people's insane beliefs can literally reshape existence—and the more people believe in something, the more real it becomes.
- Squarely in the **grimdark fantasy** tradition: think **violence, moral ambiguity, psychological horror**, and a total absence of plot armor.
- The worldbuilding is based on the concept of **Manifest Delusion**, where mental illness shapes the fabric of reality—belief systems, hallucinations, religious zeal, and self-delusions all have **tangible, magical effects**.
- This turns the book into more than just dark fantasy—it’s a **philosophical and psychological exploration** of identity, belief, and reality.
