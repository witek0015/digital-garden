*Michael L. Fletcher*
### Review
_The Mirror’s Truth_ is one of those sequels that both expands the world and frays it a little at the edges. While reading, I often felt like I was **wading through beautifully insane quicksand**, every step deeper into the plot pulled me further into confusion, but I couldn’t bring myself to look away. There’s a certain brilliance in how Michael R. Fletcher writes madness: it’s messy, uncomfortable, and incredibly compelling. But in this one, the mess sometimes threatens to eat the story whole.

With the **main trio splitting up**, the narrative scatters into multiple threads, and keeping track of who’s doing what, where, and why becomes its own challenge. The **large cast of returning characters** (many of whom are still deliciously unstable) adds to the chaos. It’s not that any of them are poorly written (they’re actually great in their derangement), but the sheer number of moving parts makes it feel like the author himself was figuring things out on the fly.

Still, when you surrender to the madness, there’s a lot to love. The **magic system**, built on belief and delusion, continues to be one of the most creative I’ve come across in fantasy. It’s so twisted and bizarre—and yet somehow **logically sound within its own rules**, which makes it all the more fun to explore. The world is broken, but it's **consistently broken**, which is a weird compliment but absolutely valid here.

In the end, _The Mirror’s Truth_ doesn’t quite reach the heights of _Beyond Redemption_, mostly because it feels more **directionless and overloaded**. But it still delivers where it counts: dark philosophy, grim violence, and a reality that bends to the whims of the insane. Not the cleanest ride, but still a **worthy descent** into the beautifully broken mess that is Manifest Delusions.
### Context
- **Sequel to _Beyond Redemption_**. Continues the story in a world where **belief still shapes reality**, but the chaos has escalated. 
- Focuses on new and returning characters grappling with **madness, power, and shifting reality**, diving deeper into the consequences of a world driven by delusion.
- Explores themes of **redemption, identity, and the fragility of truth**, all soaked in brutal violence and black humor.
