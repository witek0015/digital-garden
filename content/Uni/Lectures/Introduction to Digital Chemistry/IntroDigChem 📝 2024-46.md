# Summary:

## dr Jakub Brzeski:
- Orbital - mathematical function that describes electron position
- pi-hole - positive/partial positive charge perpendicular to the plane of that molecule
- **Symmetry Adapted Perturbation Theory (SAPT) - in SAPT, interaction energy is computed without the total energy of the monomers of dimers**
- Calculated forces are:
	- Electrostatic
	- Exchange
	- Induction
	- Dispersion
- **If the energy of the system is higher than that of monomers, they repel**
- Quantum Theory of Atoms in Molecules - by analyzing bond critical points, we can characterize the interaction. To do that, we can use laplatians
- Relativistic Quantum Mechanics - used in heavy elements
	- **The heavier the atom, the faster the inner atoms**
	- Relativity contracts orbitals of one electron atoms - 18% contraction of 1s orbital in Au
	- Lanthanide contractions - small ratio of ionic radii to atomic radii due to relativistic effects 
	- We use potential to describe all inner atoms
- Electronaffinity - how much energy is released while adding electron
