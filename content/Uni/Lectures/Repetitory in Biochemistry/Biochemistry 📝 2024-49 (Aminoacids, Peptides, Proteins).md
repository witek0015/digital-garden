## Lecture notes:
 - Peptide bond is inbetween 1 and 2 bonds lenght. It also block rotation (the drop of electrons from nitrogen int N-C bond)
 - *cis* configuration is generally not favourable energetically (only in 0.05% cases **except** proline - 6.5% cases)
 - *phi, psi* are torsion angles - dihedrals. They are defined by 4 points
	 - *phi* - 2 carbonyl carbons and two atoms in between
	 - *psi* - 2 nitrogens and 2 atoms in between
- Natural occuring peptides have limited dihedral angles - certains pairs of phi and psi correspond to certain  secondary structures, eg. *phi* = -47, *psi* = -57 is alpha helix
- **Homodetic peptide** is a cyclical polypeptide with peptide bond closing the ring
- **Heterodetic peptide** is a cyclical polypeptide with disulfide bond closing the ring
- if distance betwenn the residue *i* and residue *i+3* is shorter than 7 angstrem, we have beta turn.
