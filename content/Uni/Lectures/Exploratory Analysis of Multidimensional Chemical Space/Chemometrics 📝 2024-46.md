## Lecture notes:
- PCA - Principal Component Analysis is a dimensionality reduction technique in which high dimensional data is reduced to lower dimensional data
- Why PCA?
	- To uncover relationships between observations and variables
	- To reduce the dimensions of the data while losing as little data as possible
	- Solve the noise filtering problem
	- Eliminating multicolinearity (strong correlation between two or more variables)
- Purpose: 
	- Dimensionality reduction
	- Date exploration and evaluation
	- Linear transformation
	- Feature selection
	- Data compression
	- Clustering and Classification
